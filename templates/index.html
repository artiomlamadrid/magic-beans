Magic Beans

A modern, open-source stock analysis platform powered by Yahoo Finance data.

What can you do with Magic Beans?

  • Analyze any stock by ticker symbol (e.g., AAPL, MSFT, TSLA, etc.)
  • View comprehensive financial data including balance sheets, cash flows, earnings, dividends, and more
  • Run advanced valuation models (Discounted Cash Flow, Dividend Discount Model, P/E Analysis, and more)
  • See algorithmic buy/sell recommendations based on quantitative analysis
  • Explore analyst consensus and technical trends
  • Save and load stock data for offline analysis
  • Beautiful, responsive UI for desktop and mobile

How does it work?

  1. Enter a stock ticker in the search bar
  2. Click Analyze to fetch and process the latest data
  3. Review the results, including valuations, recommendations, and financial tables

Valuation Models Used in Magic Beans

Magic Beans uses several industry-standard quantitative models to estimate the fair value of stocks. All models are fully automated and use parameters derived from real Yahoo Finance data.

  • Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)
    Purpose: Estimates the present value of a company based on projected future free cash flows.
    Key Parameters:
    • Free Cash Flow (FCF) Growth Rate: Calculated from historical FCF trends or sector averages.
    • Projection Period: 5-10 years (configurable, default: 5 years).
    • Terminal Value: Uses the Gordon Growth Model with a conservative perpetual growth rate (default: 2%).
    • Discount Rate: Default 8% (can be adjusted for risk or sector).
    • Shares Outstanding: Pulled from latest company data.
    Logic: FCF is projected forward, discounted to present value, and summed with terminal value. The result is divided by shares outstanding to estimate fair value per share.
  • Dividend Discount Model (DDM)
    Purpose: Values a stock based on the present value of expected future dividends.
    Key Parameters:
    • Dividend Growth Rate: Estimated from historical dividend increases.
    • Discount Rate: Default 7% (can be adjusted).
    • Projection Period: 5-10 years (default: 5 years).
    • Terminal Value: Based on a perpetual growth model (default: 2%).
    Logic: Projects future dividends, discounts them to present value, and adds a terminal value for perpetuity.
  • P/E Analysis (Earnings Multiple)
    Purpose: Estimates fair value based on justified Price/Earnings ratios.
    Key Parameters:
    • Trailing and Forward EPS: Pulled from company data.
    • Justified P/E Ratio: Based on sector averages and company growth rates.
    Logic: Multiplies EPS by justified P/E to estimate fair value. Adjusts for sector premium if applicable.
  • Hypergrowth/Platform Scaling Models
    Purpose: For high-growth tech stocks, applies exponential growth and platform scaling logic.
    Key Parameters:
    • Growth Rate: Based on recent revenue/FCF acceleration.
    • Discount Rate: Higher to reflect risk (default: 10-12%).
    Logic: Uses exponential DCF and scenario analysis to capture upside potential.
Parameter Sources & Adjustments
  • All parameters are derived from the latest Yahoo Finance data for each ticker.
  • Defaults are chosen for conservatism but can be adjusted in code for advanced users.
  • Terminal values use the Gordon Growth Model with a low perpetual growth rate to avoid overestimation.
  • Discount rates are set based on market conditions and sector risk.
